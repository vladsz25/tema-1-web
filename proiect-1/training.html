Push-Pull-Legs Workout Program

An effective and balanced training split to maximize muscle growth, strength, and recovery

Day 1: Push

Focus on chest, shoulders, and triceps exercises.

Bench Press

Lie on a flat bench, grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width. Lower the bar to your chest, then press it back up. Keep your feet flat on the ground and maintain a slight arch in your lower back for proper form.

Sets: 4 sets Reps: 8-10 reps Muscle: Chest

Incline Dumbbell Press

Set an adjustable bench to about 30-45 degrees. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder level, palms facing forward. Press the weights up until arms are extended, then lower them back down.

Sets: 3 sets Reps: 10-12 reps Muscle: Upper Chest

Overhead Press

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hold a barbell at shoulder height with palms facing forward. Press the bar overhead until arms are fully extended, then lower back to starting position.

Sets: 3 sets Reps: 8-10 reps Muscle: Shoulders

Tricep Pushdowns

Stand facing a cable machine with a rope attachment. Grab the rope with both hands, keep elbows close to your body, and extend arms downward by contracting the triceps. Return to starting position with control.

Sets: 3 sets Reps: 12-15 reps Muscle: Triceps

Day 2: Pull

Focus on back, biceps, and core exercises.

Barbell Rows

Bend at the hips with a slight knee bend, keep your back straight and grab a barbell with an overhand grip. Pull the bar toward your lower chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together, then lower with control.

Sets: 4 sets Reps: 8-10 reps Muscle: Back

Pull-Ups

Hang from a pull-up bar with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width. Pull your body up until your chin clears the bar, then lower yourself with control. If needed, use assisted pull-up machines to begin.

Sets: 3 sets Reps: 8-10 reps Muscle: Back

Bicep Curls

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, hold dumbbells at your sides with palms facing forward. Curl the weights toward your shoulders while keeping elbows close to your torso, then lower with control.

Sets: 3 sets Reps: 10-12 reps Muscle: Biceps

Hanging Leg Raises

Hang from a pull-up bar with arms fully extended. Keeping legs straight, raise them until they're parallel to the floor (or higher if possible), then lower them with control. Bend knees if needed for a modified version.

Sets: 3 sets Reps: 12-15 reps Muscle: Abs

Day 3: Legs

Focus on quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves.

Squats

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, barbell resting on your upper back. Bend knees and hips to lower your body until thighs are at least parallel to the floor, then extend your legs to return to standing.

Sets: 4 sets Reps: 8-10 reps Muscle: Quads/Glutes

Romanian Deadlifts

Stand with feet hip-width apart, holding a barbell in front of your thighs. Hinge at the hips while keeping a slight bend in the knees, lower the bar toward the floor while keeping it close to your legs, then return to standing.

Sets: 3 sets Reps: 10-12 reps Muscle: Hamstrings

Leg Press

Sit in the leg press machine with feet shoulder-width apart on the platform. Release the safety bars and lower the platform by bending your knees until they form 90-degree angles, then press back up without locking your knees.

Sets: 3 sets Reps: 10-12 reps Muscle: Quads

Calf Raises

Stand on a raised platform with balls of your feet on the edge, heels hanging off. Rise up onto your toes as high as possible, then lower your heels below the platform level to stretch the calves.

Sets: 4 sets Reps: 15-20 reps Muscle: Calves

Training Tips

Workout Strategy

  • Rest 48-72 hours between training the same muscle group
  • Focus on progressive overload by gradually increasing weight
  • Maintain proper form throughout all exercises
  • Rest 1-2 minutes between sets for optimal results

Recovery

  • Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night
  • Stay hydrated throughout the day
  • Consider foam rolling for improved muscle recovery
  • Listen to your body and take extra rest days when needed
