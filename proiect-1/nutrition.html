Fuel Your Fitness Journey

High-protein recipes to maximize your workout results

Protein-Rich Recipes

Greek Yogurt Protein Bowl

Start your day with this protein-packed breakfast bowl that combines the creaminess of Greek yogurt with the crunch of nuts and the sweetness of berries.

Protein: 25g Calories: 350 kcal Time: 10 mins

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt (2% or full fat)
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup mixed berries
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds
  • 1 tablespoon sliced almonds
  • 1 scoop protein powder (optional)

Grilled Chicken & Quinoa Salad

This balanced lunch option provides complete protein from both animal and plant sources, keeping you energized throughout the day.

Protein: 35g Calories: 450 kcal Time: 25 mins

Ingredients:

  • 5 oz grilled chicken breast, sliced
  • 1/2 cup cooked quinoa
  • 2 cups mixed greens
  • 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 avocado, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil & lemon dressing

Baked Salmon with Roasted Vegetables

This dinner option delivers high-quality protein along with essential omega-3 fatty acids for improved recovery and reduced inflammation.

Protein: 30g Calories: 480 kcal Time: 30 mins

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz salmon fillet
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 medium sweet potato, cubed
  • 1/2 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Post-Workout Protein Smoothie

Refuel your muscles after an intense workout with this quick-absorbing protein smoothie that aids in recovery and muscle growth.

Protein: 30g Calories: 320 kcal Time: 5 mins

Ingredients:

  • 1 scoop whey protein powder
  • 1 banana
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 1 tablespoon peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup frozen berries
  • 1/2 cup ice cubes
  • 1 teaspoon honey (optional)

Nutrition Tips for Gym Performance

Protein Timing

Consume 20-30g of protein within 30 minutes after your workout to optimize muscle recovery and growth.

Hydration

Drink at least 3-4 liters of water daily, more on training days to maintain performance and aid recovery.

Carb Cycling

Consider higher carb intake on training days and lower carbs on rest days for improved body composition.

Meal Frequency

Aim for 4-6 smaller, balanced meals throughout the day to maintain stable energy levels and nutrient supply.
